1. Nutrition

Primary Goal: Reduce fasting glucose, LDL, VLDL, non-HDL cholesterol, and triglycerides, while optimizing vitamin D levels

Nutrition Focus Recommendations Details
Low Carbohydrate Diet High healthy fat diet (e.g., Mediterranean or Ketogenic) Aim for <50 grams of net carbs per day. Focus on leafy greens, non-starchy vegetables, avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish.
Healthy Fats Increase intake of omega-3 fatty acids Include fatty fish (salmon, mackerel) 2-4 times a week, walnuts, chia seeds. Minimize saturated fats from red meats and dairy.
Intermittent Fasting Incorporate a 16:8 fasting regimen Eat all meals within an 8-hour window (e.g., 12 PM - 8 PM). Fasting helps improve insulin sensitivity and reduces fasting glucose.
Fiber-Rich Foods Increase soluble fiber to help reduce cholesterol levels Include oatmeal, legumes, berries, and chia seeds to help lower cholesterol and stabilize blood sugar.
Vitamin D-Rich Foods Consume foods high in vitamin D Include fatty fish, fortified foods (like dairy alternatives), and egg yolks. Aim for daily sunlight exposure for 15-20 minutes.
Hydration Increase water intake to support overall health Aim for 2-3 liters of water daily to support metabolic processes and liver function.
Creatine Supplement with creatine monohydrate to support muscle mass and performance Take 3-5 grams of creatine monohydrate daily. It can be mixed in water or your preferred beverage. Best consumed post-workout or with a meal.
Protein Intake Aim for adequate protein intake to support muscle mass and metabolism Consume 1.2-1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day, focusing on lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and plant-based proteins.
Alcohol and Caffeine Limit alcohol and reduce caffeine Avoid alcohol, which can increase triglycerides and liver enzymes levels. Limit caffeine to 1-2 cups per day, preferably before noon.
